About RateSense

RateSense helps you understand what interest-rate changes mean in real dollars: monthly payments, total interest, payoff timing, and risk over time.

What you can do here

Calculate

See how a rate change affects your monthly payment and total interest for mortgages, auto loans, student loans, and credit cards.

Compare two loan options side-by-side (payment, interest, payoff time, and housing cost).

How to use it

Calculator

  1. Select a loan type.
  2. Enter your balance, rate, and length.
  3. Pick a “rate change scenario” (example: +0.50%).
  4. Click Calculate.

You can also add an extra monthly payment for amortized loans.

Mortgage housing cost

For mortgages, you can add property taxes, insurance, and monthly fees. RateSense shows your total monthly housing cost:

total housing cost = payment + (annual taxes ÷ 12) + (annual insurance ÷ 12) + monthly fees

What the results mean

Monthly payment

The scheduled payment for the loan (principal + interest). This is usually the biggest visible change when rates move.

Total interest

The total interest paid over the full payoff period (or simulated payoff period if you make extra payments).

Payoff time

How long it takes to pay off the balance based on the inputs provided.

Charts

Remaining balance

Shows how the balance falls over time. Helpful for seeing long-run cost differences.

Interest vs principal

Shows how each payment splits between interest and principal over time.

Rates

The Rates page can pull public benchmarks (like Treasury yields and mortgage survey data) so you can start from values close to today’s market.
