APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

APR is the yearly interest rate you pay. For monthly calculations, we convert it to a monthly rate (APR ÷ 12).

Why small rate changes matter

On long loans (like 30-year mortgages), even +0.25% can noticeably increase total interest over time.

Monthly vs total interest

A slightly higher monthly payment can translate into thousands more in total interest—RateSense shows both.

Fed rates vs your rate

The Fed influences short-term rates. Your loan rate depends on many factors (credit, term, lender margins), but moves often ripple through.

About

RateSense is a student-built tool that translates interest-rate changes into real household impacts: monthly payments, payoff timelines, and total interest costs.

Assumptions are simplified for education (fixed-rate amortization; basic credit-card payoff simulation). Not financial advice.
